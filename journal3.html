March 27, 2023

Community values in #computer-science

Since the last two modules, I decided to narrow my focus mainly to the #computer-science channel. I have participated continued to participate as mainly an observer but also answered and asked a few questions. At the moment of updating this entry, I don't have any special status or role in the entire server (or broader community since you can think of the server as like the main website where all the communities are accessible). I noticed that while the community is extremely active in terms of the response time to questions and the amount of users in it, there are tensions between what different people may value. From all my cumulative experience in this space, most people asking questions are seeking assistance to complete a school assignment. Those asking questions for personal projects are of a small minority. The likelihood of someone posting a question out of personal curiosity seems even smaller to me. However, most people answering questions seem to be intrinsically motivated to help others gain a better understanding of the subect content. At times when a channel is "slowing down" or there are no questions that have yet to be answered, some people will voluntarily post their experience, offering help to anyone. See the below post by a user offering their expertise:

Hi, I'm a Certified IT expert and have the following skill set if you need any help please let me know. Windows Linux Networking System Administration Cloud Engineering Cloud Computing AWS/AZURE/GCP Office 365 VOIP Technical Writing Security Ethical Hacking Programming
I have actually asked questions out of curiosity, though not in the #computer-science channel. I once asked about recommendations for philosophy literature that is application-based and was almost instantly met with enthusiastic recommendations of books (one being Practical Ethics, a great read!).

While I think the different values people hold in the community do and can lead to conflict (which I will touch on in the next section), I think this is good in the grand scheme of things. In threads of back-and-forth discourse, people asking questions are often prompted into thinking more deeply about a question. While someone may start off with a question of

Why does my code not work [insert code snippet]?
They often are guided to see the gaps in their understanding of the concepts being tested in the question and/or given useful advice about how to go about solving a similar problem independently in the future.

Communication spaces: channels vs dms

Another source of conflict I saw was with the issue of too many people asking for help directly in dms (or direct messages). While it is not uncommon for some conversations to move to dm (I have seen this occur in channels like #english where people do not want to release their writing publicly), staff in the community generally discourage this practice. This practice is discouraged not because private conversations are inherently bad for learning, but because they run the danger of people paying others to do their assignments--which is a huge violation of Homework Help's community guidelines. In fact, I saw a thread where a staff member directly clarified this (I would copy the content here, but I unfortunately cannot find the link at this moment), calling a question asker to please request assistance in the public channels. I also think this is an efficiency issue because if someone were to ask their question(s) publicly they are more likely to get multiple perspectives from multiple people instead of just relying on help from the first person who responds to their request.

My recommendations for clearer communication guidelines

I think Homework Help should make it clearer that communication should be conducted in channels. Perhaps it could set up an automated bot, like I defined in the last journal entry, which will notify posters if they mention the word dm or direct message. On the topic of bots, I am also curious about the implication of using chatGPT on such a community. I am curious what aspects are present in the interactions in #computer-science and Homework Help as a whole that cannot be replaced by chatGPT answering homework questions.
