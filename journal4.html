April 11, 2023

Spaces to interact in #computer-science

In previous journal entries, I observed that the online community for #computer-science has multiple places to interact though main interactions happen in variations of Q&A format via the #computer-science channel. I would now like to expand on that observation. I found that there are multiple places for interactions to happen such as: the main #computer-science channel, channels for general chatter, channels for events for the whole broader community (not limited to computer science) and related channels such as #uni-computer-science which is university specific computer science.

My recommendations for spaces of interaction

When I joined this online community earlier there were all the same spaces of interactions as above with the exception of the university specific channels. I recommend that the broader community has more guidelines for navigating all these spaces in general since even after being in this communtiy for so long I still find it confusing to know whether a question or topic should be put in the university specific channel or the general #computer-science channel. This observation could also be because my research and interests are in the technical side of human computer interaction, but I also think the community could do a better job of communicating norms for knowledge sharing in these different spaces. The only space where it seems clear that people should answer and ask questions are the subject specific channels such as #computer-science. In the general channels, I am always unsure what the norms for knowledge sharing are.
