How community is maintained within Homework Help

The community I am exploring is the #computer-science channel within the Homework Help Discord server. This server is very popular among students with 20k members active usually and around 165k members total. Members here usually interact asynchronously via text and at most hop on voice/video calls. There are assigned roles for people who answer questions frequently, but everything is done on a volunteer basis and moderators regularly enforce academic integrity and other community guidelines. Other than the subject content, a lot of what people learn how to do in this server is formulate a question to ask asynchronously using text + images + other (like code). Since this community is so large, I decided to focus on the computer science learning community within the Homework Help community. To do this, I observed the following channels (which are similar to Slack channels:

  1. #computer-science
  2. #c-cpp
  3. #java-jvm
  4. #python
  5. #js-ts
  6. #cs-other-languages
  7. #web-development
  8. #computer-engineering
  9. #latex

The main platform used for this community is Discord which is a collaboration platform originally developed to allow people who play video games to connect. However, over the past few years Discord has been adapted for educational uses--particularly in computer science given the lower barrier to entry since many computer science students are technically literate and for online classes during the pandemic. In fact, many people refer to Slack as the "professional" version of Discord. As a researcher, I am very interested in this phenomenon and also have personal experience using Discord professionally while previously working in a startup, as a student, and as a teaching instructor. I would be happy to share resources if anyone is interested in learning more about the adaptive uses of Discord in non-video gaming settings (educational, recreational, etc.).

Implications for Moderation

The community operates like a living-forum. If we think of traditional forms like question and answer bulletin boards, the Homework Help server is built on those basic components ... plus a whole lot more. Members mainly interact in these specific channels, but there is also a #general channel for casual conversation with everyone and a #introductions channel for new joiners to introduce themselves. There are also channels for #tips and #rules to guide new members in learning about the structure and expectations of the community. Members of the community can participate by asking questions, answering questions, simply reading what others ask, and reacting with emojis or gifs. Members can select roles by their subject area of interest or expertise. There are also roles assigned such as Contact Staff and Valued Contributor. Staff are volunteer moderators who regularly screen the channels for harmful messages or spam. In addition to Valued Contributors, there are many more roles (so many that I'll probably expand on this in a later journal entry) which award people who have put in varying levels of time and effort into helping answer others questions and participating in discussions in the channels.

My experience asking and answering questions

Above, I talked about how community is sustained by the moderators and the people who keep asking questions. Community is created by the relationships formed among question askers and answerers. Due to the size of the server, it can be difficult to form 1-1 relationships, however, relationships seem to form over the span of a few days or a few interactions when a person posts and question and another person responds via a thread (like Twitter threads for those unfamiliar). The asker will usually follow-up and express their gratitude. This gratitude and follow-up is key since that's when the relationship transitions from ask-answer to more of a back-and-forth conversation. Of course, I'm simplifying many of the interactions here since most conversations involve 2+ parties and many streams of back and forth that can happen all in one day or over the span of several days.
