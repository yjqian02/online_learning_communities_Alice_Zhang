Q&A vs Tutors: Tensions in #computer-science in Homework Help

For the past two weeks (modules 5 & 6), I decided to narrow my focus mainly to the #computer-science channel. I have participated mainly as an observer but also answered and asked a few questions. I don't have any special status or role in the entire server (or broader community since you can think of the server as like the main website where all the communities are accessible). I noticed that while the community is extremely active in terms of the response time to questions and the amount of users in it, there is a unique tension between the question-answer forum interpretation of the community and the online-tutor interpretation of it. To futher elaborate, a user posted the following:

I love the classic 1. Ask for help 2. Not provide a question 3. Someone responds immediately 4. Person never messages again
The above post was met with responding support, showing that this is a common occurrence on these types of platforms. This example also interestingly shows that while the initial relationship between the asker and answerer is tentative and fragile with little accountability on mostly the asker's side, the answerer is often able to form relationships with others in the community just by responding to offer their help. This was also surprising to me since I would intuitively expect that is there more of a shortage of answerers than askers, not for it to be the other way around.

Implications for Moderation

To maintain the community, there are a series of "bots" that roam around each of the channels. Bots are essentially pieces of software written to perform small tasks in a specific environment. In this case, for example, there is a "rewardbot" that will automatically react to messages that show thanks for helpers with cheerful emojis. There are also Bots that automatically warn users for posting inappropriate content. I have yet to seen egregiously inappropriate content, and I assume that is the doing of the bots and the human volunteer moderators. Another interesting aspect of maintenance is that of "spam" or multiple posts. Some users will often just message "help" or send the same question to multiple channels. In this case, I usually see these messages taken down after multiple people respond the tell the user this behavior violates community norms. I find this particularly interesting because while this isn't spam in the case of advertisement, it seems like these users need to be better educated on the community norms. So, I'm curious about what happens to their accounts after they do this. Are they kicked out of the server or just given a stern warning? If they are kicked out, will they eventually come back with another account?

My experience asking and answering questions

I also dabbled a bit in asking and answering questions. For this, I actually branched out of #computer-science because I was personally curious about a topic in #philosophy. I did answer a few #computer-science and #english questions though. My experience was largely positive. When I asked questions people responded quickly and were very kind and helpful. I was redirected to credible resources like books written by experts. When I thanked people, they also either replied and/or used emojis to show that they recognized my thanks. Personally, I find that strengthens the relationship between asker and answerer since it doesn't feel like a "one-and-done" situation then. When answering questions I always received thank you messages as well.
